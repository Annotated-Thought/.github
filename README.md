# Annotated Thought

> *Making the invisible visible — one reasoning step at a time.*

Welcome to **Annotated Thought**, an organization dedicated to illuminating the *how* and *why* behind ideas in artificial intelligence and machine learning. We believe that understanding a result is only half the picture. The other half is the reasoning that got you there.

---

## What We Do

Most explanations in AI/ML tell you what happens. We annotate **the thinking**.

Our work breaks complex concepts, architectures, and phenomena into richly documented walkthroughs — exposing the chain of decisions, assumptions, and intuitions that underlie them. Each piece is designed to be read like a conversation with a careful thinker, not a textbook.

Whether it's tracing why a particular training dynamic emerges, unpacking the geometry of an attention mechanism, or reconstructing the reasoning behind a design choice — we make the process explicit.

---

## Our Philosophy

- **Reasoning is a first-class artifact.** The thought process matters as much as the conclusion.
- **Depth over breadth.** We'd rather cover one concept thoroughly than many concepts shallowly.
- **Rigor without opacity.** Precision and clarity are not in conflict — we pursue both.
- **Built for researchers.** Our content assumes technical fluency and rewards careful reading.

---

## What You'll Find Here

- **Annotated walkthroughs** of ML concepts, training dynamics, and architectural decisions
- **Step-by-step reasoning traces** that make implicit assumptions explicit
- **Deep dives** into topics where the standard explanation leaves too much unsaid

---

## Getting Involved

We welcome researchers who share the conviction that understanding *how* to think through a problem is as valuable as any result.

- Browse our repositories to explore current work
- Open an issue to suggest a topic, flag an error, or start a discussion
- Submit a pull request if you'd like to contribute an annotation or correction

---

## A Note on the Name

*Annotated Thought* is intentional. An annotation is more than a comment — it is an act of making latent reasoning legible. Our goal is to do that for the ideas that shape modern AI research.
